Alexander and the

Terrible, Horrible,
No Good, Very Bad

Data Breach

A cautionary tale for modern security teams

Alexander woke up with
gum in his hair.

And a calendar full of meetings.

And a very urgent email that said:

"⚠️ Password expires in 10 minutes."

Alexander was running late.

So Alexander clicked it.

That was the beginning of a terrible, horrible, no good, very bad breach.

❧ 1 ❧

Nothing happened.

Alexander thought this was excellent news.

But somewhere far away...

a hacker now had Alexander's password.

And Alexander's MFA session.

And Alexander's very bad luck.

❧ 2 ❧

At work, everything
felt normal.

Alexander answered emails.

Joined meetings.

Ate a very disappointing salad.

Meanwhile...

the hacker logged in.

Very quietly.

Very rudely.

❧ 3 ❧

The hacker searched
the company's files.

They found:

contracts

employee records

financial documents

customer data

product plans

and a folder labeled:

🚫 DO NOT OPEN

The hacker opened it immediately.

Alexander would have too.

❧ 4 ❧

The hacker
downloaded everything.

Then downloaded more things.

Then downloaded things they didn't even need.

Hackers can be greedy.

❧ 5 ❧

Then the hacker
shared files
with another hacker.

Who shared them with another hacker.

Who uploaded them somewhere terrible.

Alexander hated chain collaboration.

❧ 6 ❧

At 2:14 AM,

someone noticed unusual activity.

At 2:15 AM,

security got an alert.

At 2:16 AM,

everyone's phones started ringing.

At 2:17 AM,

Alexander wished he had become a baker.

❧ 7 ❧

The legal team asked:
"What exactly was stolen?"

The security team checked the logs.

The logs said:

¯\_(ツ)_/¯

This was not helpful.

❧ 8 ❧

The compliance team asked:
"Can we prove
what was accessed?"

The answer was:

"Not exactly."

The compliance team became dramatically less cheerful.

❧ 9 ❧

Then customers
started calling.

Then reporters started calling.

Then the board started calling.

Alexander stopped answering unknown numbers.

❧ 10 ❧

The board asked:
"How did this happen?"

Alexander said:

"That is a longer story
than this book allows."

❧ 11 ❧

The company said:
"But we have
security tools!"

And they did.

Tools for endpoints.

Tools for networks.

Tools for identities.

Tools for finding sensitive files.

But not enough protection followed the data once it moved.

That was a problem.

❧ 12 ❧

So Alexander made
a better plan:

  • Find sensitive data.
  • Control who touches it.
  • Protect the data itself.
  • Keep protection attached wherever it travels.
  • And never trust urgent password emails.
❧ 13 ❧

Soon there were fewer
midnight calls.

Fewer headlines.

Fewer panic meetings.

And far fewer terrible, horrible, no good, very bad breaches.

Alexander still hated phishing emails.

But things got much better.

✨ The End. ✨
